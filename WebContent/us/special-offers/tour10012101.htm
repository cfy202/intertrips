1
USA / Beijing
Depart USA for China while enjoying delicious meals, drinks, and in-flight entertainment during your journey.
2
Arrive in Beijing
Upon your arrival, our guide will meet you at the airport and then escort you to check into your luxury hotel.
3
Beijing (AB)
Spend the day free at leisure or take the optional tour to visit the Forbidden City & Summer Palace. Start the day with Tiananmen Square, which is noted as the largest public square in the world and then the Imperial Palace in the Forbidden City. Stop at a Pearl Factory to gain a little knowledge of fresh pearls. Later visit Summer Palace declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1998. In the evening, don't miss the chance to taste Beijing's most delicious Peking Duck(Optional).
4
Beijing (AB)
Spend the day free at leisure or take an optional tour to visit the Great Wall of China, one of the seven greatest man-made wonders of the world and is definitely the focal point of China. You will see and perhaps climb the Wall and enjoy the marvelous mountain views. Stop by a local Jade Museum to learn the history of Jade and enjoy lunch before heading to the Ming Tombs, where thirteen of sixteen Ming Dynasty emperors are buried. This evening, take the opportunity to enjoy the Musical performance of The Golden Mask Dynasty Show(Optional), a large-scale Chinese musical drama with ancient romantic legend and the marvelous Chinese art.
5
Beijing (AB)
Today we continue our journey by flying to Xian, the capital of Shaanxi Province, one of the oldest cities in China. Xian is one of the Four Great Ancient Capitals of China with more than 3100 years of history. The city was known as "Chang'an" before Ming Dynasty. Xian is the eastern terminus of the Silk Road and home to the Terra Cotta Warriors. Upon arrival in Xian, your first stop will be at the Buddhist Jianfu Temple, where the towering Small Wild Goose Pagoda commands panoramic views of the city.
6
Beijing - Shanghai (AB)✈
Free day at leisure.
Spend the day free or take the optional tour to witness the famous "Terra Cotta Warriors." The serendipitous discovery of the Terra Cotta Warriors in 1974 has drawn throngs of visitors to Xian each year and ranks as one of the world's most important recent archaeological finds. Three excavation pits have been opened to the public with a fourth underway. Spend some time admiring the fine troops, horses and carriages in battle formation. Next you'll head to the Terracotta Replication Factory that actually re-made terracotta soldiers. After lunch, the motor coach takes us back inside city limits to visit the North Square of Big Wild Goose Pagoda, a square built in Tang style as well as the largest fountain square in Asia.
7
Shanghai (AB)
Spend the day free at leisure or take the optional tours. You will start the tour with a visit the Jade Buddha Temple, built during the Qing Dynasty. It is famous for two beautiful white-jade Buddha. Continue to Nanjing Road and walk at the Shanghai Bund, the famus promenade and regarded as the gallery of Worldwide Architecture. Stop by a gem Exhibition Center for some shopping before heading to Yu Garden, the most beautiful garden built during the Ming Dynasty. Afterwards, enjoy some free time at the Old City Bazaar (City god Temple) area. Or take an optional excursion tour to Suzhou, and visit the Lingerling Garden, Grand canal and the ancient street-Shan tang Street, with a history of about 1,100 years, Its layout and architecture are bet best to represent the characteristics of Suzhou's old alleys. We will visit the Embroidery Research Institute to enjoy the fancy Chinese art before heading back to Shanghai.
8
Shanghai (AB)
Take this free day to explore Shanghai any way you wish - visit the staggeringly ancient displays at the Shanghai Museum, search for last-minute souvenirs in Yuyuan Market, or arrange to watch a gasp-inducing show of Chinese acrobatics.
9
Shanghai - USA (AB)
Today you'll be escorted to the airport to board your homeward flight. Service ends here.

Day 03

Beijing

Forbidden City & Summer Palace with lunch

$55

Peking Duck Dinner with Local Red Wine

Peking Duck Dinner, Beijing's most delicious food, is a must to experience when you come to Beijing. You will also taste the local red wine while enjoying the delicious Peking Duck Dinner. The culinary of Peking duck can be dated back to Yuan dynasty when it was a dish of imperial food. Peking Duck is prepared from specially-bred Beijing crammed duck with a unique roasting process which gives it a perfect combination of color, aroma and taste, a crisp thin skin and a mouth-melting and delicious flavor. The meat is eaten with scallion, cucumber and sweet bean sauce with pancakes rolled around the fillings. Sometimes pickled radish is also inside, and other sauces can be used. You may also have the chance to enjoy the culinary presentation as the chef roasts the duck directly over the fire and then slices the meat on table side.

$35
Day 04

Beijing

Great Wall and Ming Tombs with lunch

$59

The Golden Mask Dynasty

The Golden Mask Dynasty is a large-scale Chinese musical drama, performed at the OCT Theater in Beijing Happy Valley. The performance is fairy-tale like story of war, royal banquets, and romance. With the Ancient Sanxingdui Civilization as times background, it tells the story of the beautiful Golden Mask Queen who built a glorious kingdom with her wisdom, tolerance, trust and love. The performance has eight chapters and included more than 200 actors from China and abroad. Produced by Chinese playwrights, directors and designers, the play features Chinese dances, acrobatics, costumes, and lighting and acoustics. Coming here, you will be fully immersed in the ancient romantic legend and the marvelous Chinese art.

Please book this optional tour with your local tour guide in China

$49
Day 06

Shanghai

Shanghai Lujiazui FTDZ & Huang Pu River Cruise

Located on the east bank of the Huangpu River in Pudong, across from the Bund, Lujiazui Finance and Trade Zone is the key financial center in Shanghai. It is surrounded by the Oriental Pearl Tower, Jin Mao Tower, Shanghai Tower, Shanghai World Financial Center and a large number of business buildings. Huangpu River is the city's shipping artery both to the East China Sea and the mouth of the Yangtze River. Huangpu River Cruise is just one of the best ways to have a look both old and new Shanghai. You'll see the magnificent building skylines on both the historic west side (the Bund), and the modern east side (Pudong). A spectacular view is that the two suspension bridges, Nanpu Bridge and Yangpu Bridge.
